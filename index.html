Choosing a Model

How businesses decide.

Choosing the right cloud service model depends on your team's priorities. Do you need complete control? Rapid deployment? Maximum simplicity? Each model answers a different question. Most enterprises use all three: IaaS for infrastructure-heavy workloads, PaaS for developer productivity, and SaaS for productivity tools. Below is the decision tree your organization likely uses.

01
Need full control?
Choose IaaS. Custom OS, runtimes, network rules, databases. You build from raw infrastructure up. Best for startups, compliance-heavy orgs, and teams with deep DevOps expertise who need to optimize every layer.
02
Just ship code?
Choose PaaS. Deploy apps without managing servers, databases, or OS patches. The platform handles scaling, monitoring, and infrastructure. Ideal for dev teams that want speed without infrastructure headaches.
03
Ready-to-use software?
Choose SaaS. Open a browser and start working. No installation, no maintenance, no patching. Perfect for end-users, small teams, and organizations that want the lowest operational burden.

Real-World IaaS

Three providers, one model.

IaaS is offered by the major cloud providers, each with different strengths. AWS EC2 dominates market share and offers the broadest feature set. Google Compute Engine is known for superior networking and per-second billing. Azure integrates seamlessly with Microsoft enterprise software like Active Directory. All three provide bare-metal control — you choose your OS, runtime, and applications.

AWS EC2
Largest ecosystem. Thousands of instance types, custom AMIs, deep integration with AWS services. Used by startups and enterprises worldwide.
Google Compute Engine
Best networking & data handling. Per-second billing, advanced networking, world-class data analytics integration.
Azure VMs
Enterprise Windows integration. Native Active Directory, hybrid cloud setup, Office 365 integration. Dominant in large enterprises.

Real-World PaaS

Platforms that let you just code.

PaaS removes infrastructure management entirely. You push code, and the platform handles servers, databases, scaling, and deployment. Google App Engine is serverless-first and multilingual. Heroku is the simplest entry point for startups—famous for `git push heroku main`. Azure App Service integrates with Microsoft tools and is strong in enterprise environments. Choose based on your tech stack and deployment workflow.

Google App Engine
Serverless deployment. Deploy Python, Java, Go, Node.js, or Ruby. Auto-scales from zero to thousands of requests. Pay per CPU-millisecond.
Heroku
Git-first deployment. `git push heroku main` deploys instantly. Managed Postgres, Redis, add-ons ecosystem. Favorite for startup MVPs and side projects.
Azure App Service
Enterprise-grade platform. Built-in CI/CD, integrated with Azure DevOps and Microsoft 365. Windows and Linux support, auto-scaling, staging slots.

Software as a Service

SaaS.

The provider manages everything — infrastructure, platform, and the application itself. You simply open a browser and use it.

01
Access from any device
Browser-based. Works on laptops, tablets, phones — no install.
02
Always up to date
Provider pushes updates. You never patch or upgrade manually.
03
Subscription pricing
Monthly or annual plans. No license keys, no upfront cost.

SaaS You Already Use

Software you never installed.

SaaS is the software you use every day in the browser. You don't install anything. You don't manage a database. You don't patch it. Just open your browser, log in, and start working. Updates happen seamlessly in the background. It's the easiest cloud model to adopt because there's zero infrastructure knowledge required.

Gmail
Email, calendar, mobile. 1.8 billion users worldwide. Zero setup — just log in.
Google Workspace
Docs, Sheets, Slides, Drive. Collaborate in real-time with anyone, anywhere.
Salesforce
CRM platform. Manages billions in customer relationships globally. The original SaaS pioneer.
Zoom
Video conferencing. Join meetings from a browser link. No client required.
